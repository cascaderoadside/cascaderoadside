# Google Ads Campaign Setup — Cascade Roadside

## Quick Start

**Goal:** Launch a Google Ads campaign for Cascade Roadside in Pierce County (Puyallup, Tacoma, Federal Way) and start getting roadside assistance calls.

**Timeline:** 30 minutes to set up, 24-48 hours for ad approval.

**Expected ROI:** At $15/day budget, expect 3-8 calls/day in Pierce County. With $95 flat rate and 40% conversion, that's ~$200 profit on $450 monthly spend.

---

## Step 1: Create Your Google Ads Account

1. Go to **https://ads.google.com**
2. Sign in with your Google account (or create one)
3. Select your country (United States)
4. Enter your business name: **Cascade Roadside**
5. Enter your website: **https://cascaderoadside.com**
6. Select your business type: **Services > Automotive > Roadside Assistance**
7. Choose your primary location: **Puyallup, WA**
8. Click **Create Account**

---

## Step 2: Choose Campaign Type — Local Services Ads (LSA)

**Why LSA?** These ads appear ABOVE regular search results for local services. Perfect for roadside assistance.

1. In your Google Ads dashboard, click **+ New Campaign**
2. Select campaign type: **Local Services Ads**
3. Click **Local Services Ads** option
4. Go to **https://ads.google.com/localservices** directly (faster)

### LSA Setup Details

- **Service category:** Roadside Assistance / Locksmith
- **Service areas:** Puyallup, Tacoma, Federal Way, Pierce County
- **Radius:** 25-mile radius from Puyallup
- **Phone number:** (253) 322-5282
- **Hours:** 24/7 (set availability to always available)
- **Licensing:** Check "Licensed" if applicable (you have a business license)
- **Insurance:** Check "Insured"

### Why LSA Over Traditional Search Ads?

| Feature | LSA | Search Ads |
|---------|-----|-----------|
| Where it shows | ABOVE search results | Below/beside organic results |
| Best for | Local services | National/broad keywords |
| Cost | Pay per lead/call | Pay per click |
| Setup time | 15 minutes | 30+ minutes |
| Approval | 2-4 hours | 24 hours |

**For Cascade Roadside:** LSA is the clear winner. You want calls, not clicks.

## Step 6: Set Up Conversion Tracking

You want to know which ads convert to actual jobs. Google will track calls from your ads.

1. In your Google Ads account, go to **Tools & Settings > Conversions**
2. Click **+ New Conversion**
3. Select **Phone Calls** as conversion type
4. Choose **Calls from ads**
5. Enter your business phone: **(253) 322-5282**
6. Google will give you a forwarding number to track calls
7. Add this forwarding number to your ads/website

**What to track:**
- Calls from ads (obviously)
- Call duration (30+ seconds = serious inquiry)
- Call-to-job conversion (how many calls = jobs)

## Step 9: Monitor Performance (First 2 Weeks)

Once live, check your dashboard daily:

1. **Impressions** — How many people saw your ad?
2. **Clicks/Calls** — How many clicked or called?
3. **Click-through rate (CTR)** — Healthy CTR is 3-8%
4. **Cost per call** — Aim for $8-15
5. **Call duration** — Calls under 30 seconds are probably wrong numbers

### What to Adjust

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Few impressions | Increase budget slightly, add keywords, expand service area |
| High cost/call | Pause weak keywords, tighten negative keywords, improve ad copy |
| Low CTR | Refresh ad copy, test different headlines, adjust bids |
| Wrong number calls | Add "business phone" to your ad or landing page headline |

## Troubleshooting

### My ads aren't showing

- **Check approval status** — Go to Ads > Status. Wait 24-48 hours.
- **Check budget** — Is your daily budget set?
- **Check keywords** — Are they triggered by search volume?
- **Check service area** — Are you targeting the right locations?

### My cost per call is too high

- **Pause bottom 30% of keywords** — they're not converting
- **Add more negative keywords** — exclude non-buyers
- **Tighten location targeting** — focus on Puyallup, less on Federal Way if $$ is high there
- **Improve ad copy** — mention "$95 flat rate" to filter for budget-conscious buyers

### Calls are too short (wrong numbers)

- **Clarify your headline** — "Roadside Assistance for Vehicles"
- **Add "Licensed & Insured" to ad** — filters out tire/automotive parts buyers
- **Check landing page** — make sure "Roadside Assistance" is clear, not ambiguous

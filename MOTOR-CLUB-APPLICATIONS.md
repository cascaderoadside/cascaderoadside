## 💡 NOTES ON PAY RATES

**Why Agero Pays Higher ($45–$65 vs. Allstate's $20–25)**

1. **Agero handles volume** — 12M+ dispatches/year across 100+ programs
   - They can negotiate lower per-call costs with contractors because volume is predictable
   - They pay better to attract quality providers in competitive markets

2. **Service mix** — Agero's network includes:
   - Insurance carriers (higher margins)
   - Auto brands (Ford Roadside, GM OnStar, etc.)
   - Motor clubs (AAA at scale)
   - Each pays Agero differently, allowing them to pass through premium rates to providers

3. **Performance incentives** — Better networks reward:
   - Fast response times
   - High customer satisfaction
   - Availability during peak hours
   - This can push rates up to $65+/call for top performers

**Realistic Expectations:**
- Jump Start: $45–$60
- Lockout: $50–$65
- Fuel Delivery: $45–$60
- Tire Change: $50–$70
- (Towing would be higher, but you're not offering it)

**Monthly Income Estimate** (if you handle 10 calls/week):
- Low: 40 calls/month × $45 = **$1,800/month**
- High: 40 calls/month × $65 = **$2,600/month**
- This is gross; subtract fuel, vehicle wear, insurance

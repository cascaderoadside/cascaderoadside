What Causes a Dead Battery?

Understanding why your battery died can help prevent it in the future:

🔌 Left Lights On

Headlights, interior lights, or dashboard lights left on overnight drain the battery.

❄️ Cold Weather

Batteries lose power in cold temperatures. Winter mornings are prime dead-battery season.

🔧 Old Battery

Most car batteries last 3–5 years. An aging battery may fail without warning.

⚡ Faulty Alternator

Your alternator charges the battery while driving. If it fails, the battery won't recharge.

🔌 Parasitic Drain

Some electrical devices draw power even when the car is off, slowly draining the battery.

🅰️ Loose Connections

Corroded or loose battery terminals prevent proper charging and starting.

⚡ Safe Jump-Start Tips

Don't Attempt DIY Jumps with Other Cars

Wrong cable connections can damage both vehicles. Let professionals handle it.

Turn Off All Electronics

Before the jump, turn off lights, radio, and other electronics to protect sensitive components.

Let the Battery Charge

After a jump start, drive for 20+ minutes to let your alternator recharge the battery.

Get Your Battery Tested

A dead battery often signals a deeper problem. Visit a mechanic for diagnosis and replacement if needed.
